Yoghurt project

Yoghurt is a semi-solid, fermented dairy product with a smooth texture and a mildly sour flavor. It is produced by adding specific live bacterial cultures (primarily Lactobacillus bulgaricus and Streptococcus thermophilus) to milk. The process, known as fermentation, involves these bacteria converting the milk's natural sugar (lactose) into lactic acid. This acid causes the milk proteins to thicken and gives yoghurt its characteristic tangy taste and preserved shelf life. Yoghurt is widely recognized as a "superfood" due to several key benefits: it contains probiotics (live cultures) that balance gut bacteria. It is a rich source of calcium, phosphorus, and often Vitamin D, which are essential for maintaining bone density and preventing osteoporosis. Also, because the fermentation process breaks down lactose, many people who are lactose intolerant find yoghurt much easier to digest than plain milk.

Weather App

The Weather app is a responsive web application that displays current weather information for any location. It uses a weather API to fetch real-time data, including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and weather conditions. The app features a clean and intuitive user interface with a modern design that adapts to different screen sizes. Users can search for weather information by entering a city name or using their current location. The app also includes a 5-day forecast, allowing users to plan their activities based on upcoming weather conditions. The Weather project is built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and it demonstrates my ability to create functional and visually appealing web applications.
